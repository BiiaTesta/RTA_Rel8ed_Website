Here's a quick guide on how to get started with Power BI Online:


(1) Upload your dataset

  • Go to the Home tab and select Get Data.
  • Choose Upload your file and make sure it's in the correct format.
  • Once uploaded, your dataset will be visible in the Fields pane on the right side of the screen.

(2) Visualizations

  • Select a visualization type (Bar chart, Pie chart, Line graph, etc.) from the Visualizations pane.
  • Drag and drop the appropriate fields from your dataset into the Values, Axis, or Legend areas to populate the visualization.

(3) Filter Your Data

  • Use the Filter Pane on the right side to apply filters and narrow down your data.

(4) Create Calculations

  • If needed, go to the Data view to create calculated columns or measures that help answer your questions.

(5) Format Your Visuals

  • Click on the chart or visual and use the Format tab to adjust the appearance and make it presentable.


FAQ


Having issues creating Power BI accounts using non-Outlook or school e-mails?

This is a known limitation, but there are a few alternatives we can explore to get you started:


Option 1: Use Your School Email Address

If you have access to a school-provided email (e.g., @edu.com), please use that to sign up for Power BI. If the school email is not being accepted, it might be due to a licensing issue. In that case, let me know, and I'll work on a solution with our IT team.

Option 2: Create a Free Outlook Account

If your school email is not working, you can create a free Outlook account specifically for Power BI. Here's how:

  • Visit Outlook.com and sign up for a new email address.
  • Once you have the Outlook email, use it to sign up for Power BI.

Option 3: Use Power BI Desktop Application

If creating an online Power BI service account is not possible, you can download and use the Power BI Desktop application instead. This version does not require sign-in and has all the functionalities needed to work on your data visualizations.

  • Download Power BI Desktop.
  • Work on your projects locally and share the .pbix files with the class.

Option 4: Use a Business or Work Email (If Available)

  • If needed, go to the Data view to create calculated columns or measures that help answer your questions.
